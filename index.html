Chase Lyall

Objective

Hello, my name is Chase Lyall, and I am a business and technology consultant for Accenture Federal Services. I am especially interested in how large datasets can improve politics and am therefore working to better understand big data analysis and large-scale web apps and the technologies behind them (Linux, Kubernetes and Docker, Nginx, etc.), cloud computing (Google Cloud, AWS, Azure), databases (Oracle 12c, NoSQL), data analysis (Matplotlib, Pandas, R), data visualization (D3.js, Google Charts, Tableau), web scraping (Selenium), and more.

Interests

Outside of work I enjoy catching up on the news via NPR One (I highly suggest it and so does NPR, they will not stop suggesting it), BBC, Google News, and Reddit. I also enjoy exercising, which is currently mostly comprised of rock climbing, weight lifting, running, and biking. And on rare occations I especially enjoy travelling so that I can learn about other cultures and food, and because it is hard to hate up close. My other interests include skiing or snowboarding, hiking and camping, and volunteering for the homeless. Besides NPR One I highly suggest HBO's Last Week Tonight with John Oliver, NPR's This American Life, NPR's Wait Wait Don't Tell Me, CBS's 60 Minutes, and Google Photos.

Current Issues

Here are some current issues that interst me: I would like to work to eliminate gerrymandering, repeal Citizens United and outlaw SuperPACs, switch from "first past the post" voting to a "single transferrable vote" voting method, fight climate change by utilizing clean energy, increasing recycling, and reducing over-consumption and overpopulation, expand education and reduce tuition expenses, reduce the national defecit, expand the Earned Income Tax Credit, reduce healthcare expenditures by increasing transparency and efficiency, promote family planning and contraception globally, reduce homelessness and poverty, increase the Social Security tax limit, reduce straw man purchases of firearms, utilize background checks for every firearm sale, outlaw the death penalty, outlaw for-profit prisons, and switch from race-associated polling methods to income and location based research metrics.
